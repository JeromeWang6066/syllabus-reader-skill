# Syllabus Section Patterns & Terminology

This reference covers common syllabus structures across university systems worldwide.
The examples are drawn from major university systems and represent universal patterns
found across regions — section headers, grading terminology, and risk indicators.
Use this reference as a guide, not a rulebook: every university has its own conventions.
When you encounter an unfamiliar format, look for the underlying structure rather than
expecting an exact keyword match.

International joint programs and English-taught courses in Chinese universities
often mix both conventions. Look for:
- Bilingual headers: "课程简介 / Course Description"
- Grading on Chinese scale but described in English
- Mix of Chinese and English textbook references

## Grading Structure Recognition Patterns

### Chinese University Typical Breakdown
```
平时成绩 (Coursework): 30-40%
  ├── 考勤 (Attendance): 5-10%
  ├── 作业 (Assignments): 10-20%
  └── 课堂参与 (Participation): 5-10%
期末考试 (Final Exam): 60-70%
实验 (Lab): 10-20% (if applicable)
```

### US University Typical Breakdown
```
Assignments/Homework: 20-30%
Midterm Exam(s): 20-30%
Final Exam: 25-35%
Participation: 5-15%
Projects/Papers: 15-25%
Quizzes: 5-15%
```

### UK University Typical Breakdown
```
Coursework/Essays: 40-60%
Exam: 40-60%
Presentation: 10-20%
Participation: 5-10%
```

## Date Format Patterns

Common date expressions found in syllabi:
- ISO: 2026-09-15, 2026/09/15
- US: September 15, 2026; 9/15/2026; Sep 15
- Chinese: 2026年9月15日; 9月15日
- Week-based: Week 3 (Sep 15-19); 第三周
- Relative: "First Monday after add/drop"; "Last class before break"

When parsing week-based schedules, note:
- Chinese semesters typically run Week 1 through Week 16-18
- US semesters run Week 1 through Week 15 + Finals Week
- UK terms run Week 1 through Week 10-12 (per term)

# Critical Risk Indicators & Potential Pitfalls (特别关注 / 避坑识别模式)

This section covers syllabus patterns that flag hidden risks and policies students often
overlook. Use these patterns during the risk scan pass to identify and flag critical items.

---

## A. Attendance-Based Failure Triggers (出勤挂科红线)

### US Patterns
```
"Missing more than [N] classes will result in an automatic F"
"After [N] unexcused absences, your final grade will be reduced by one letter grade per additional absence"
"Accumulation of [N] or more absences (excused or unexcused) constitutes excessive absence and will result in failure"
"Students who miss more than [N]% of class meetings will receive a failing grade"
"If you have [N] or more absences, you will not be eligible to pass"
"Three late arrivals count as one absence"
"Leaving early without permission counts as an absence"
```

### Chinese Patterns
```
"缺课 [N] 次取消考试资格"
"缺勤累计达到 [N] 次按不及格处理"
"旷课 [N] 节以上取消考核资格"
"缺课超过总课时 [N]% 者不得参加期末考试"
"迟到 [N] 次按旷课一节计算"
"无故缺课 [N] 次不予补考"
"三次迟到算一次旷课"
```

### Detection Keywords
- US: "automatic F", "result in failure", "not eligible to pass", "administrative withdrawal", "excessive absence"
- CN: "取消考试资格", "按不及格处理", "不予补考", "不得参加", "取消考核"

## B. Strict Prerequisite Enforcement (严格前置要求)

### US Patterns
```
"Prerequisite: [COURSE] with a grade of C- or better"
"Students must have completed [COURSE] (grade of B or above)"
"Enforced prerequisite — students without [COURSE] will be administratively dropped"
"Department permission required for enrollment without prerequisite"
"Placement exam score of [X] or AP score of [Y] required"
"Co-requisite: must be enrolled concurrently in [COURSE]"
```

### Chinese Patterns
```
"先修课程：[COURSE]"
"必须先修 [COURSE] 方可选修本课程"
"预修要求：[COURSE] 成绩不低于 [X] 分"
"未修 [COURSE] 者不得选课"
"同修要求：须同时选修 [COURSE]"
"前置课：[COURSE]"
```

### Detection Keywords
- US: "prerequisite", "must have completed", "grade of C or better", "enforced", "administratively dropped"
- CN: "先修", "前置课", "必须先修", "预修要求", "未修X不得选课"

### Distinguishing "Recommended" vs "Required"
- "Recommended background" / "建议先修" → advisory only, mark as [MEDIUM] or don't flag
- "Prerequisite" / "先修要求" → enforced, mark as [CRITICAL]
- "Strictly enforced" / "必须满足" → mark as [CRITICAL]
- If the language is ambiguous (e.g. "students are expected to have..."), flag as [MEDIUM]

## C. Workload Collision Detection (任务集中碰撞)

### Detection Strategy
Cross-reference the weekly schedule with:
1. Assignment due dates
2. Exam dates
3. Project milestones
4. Presentation dates
5. Lab report deadlines

Flag any week where 3+ major items converge. Also check for:

### Pre/Post-Break Collapse
```
Week before Thanksgiving → often has midterm + project milestone
Week after Spring Break → multiple deadlines often pile up
国庆前一周 → 往往有期中考试
五一前后 → 任务密集
```

### Reading Week Traps
```
"Reading Week" → check if it's actually a project work week with deliverables due right after
"Study Week" → verify it's not filled with make-up classes or review sessions
"复习周" → 确认是否有补课或突击考试
```

### Back-to-Back Deadlines
```
Flag: Two major deadlines within 48 hours of each other
Flag: Final project due the same week as the final exam
Flag: Three problem sets due in a single week
```

## D. Grade Threshold Traps (评分门槛陷阱)

### US Patterns — Double-Pass Rule
```
"You must pass both the lecture and lab components to pass the course"
"You must earn at least a C- on the final exam to pass regardless of your overall average"
"Failure to complete all major assignments will result in a failing grade"
"Students scoring below 60% on the final will receive an F for the course"
"A minimum score of 50% on the final exam is required to pass the module"
"All major assignments must be submitted to be eligible for a passing grade"
```

### Chinese Patterns
```
"期末考试不及格则总评不及格（一票否决）"
"卷面成绩低于 [X] 分者总评按不及格处理"
"实验和理论课均须及格"
"各项考核成绩均须达到 [X] 分"
"缺交作业超过 [N] 次取消考试资格"
"总评成绩 = 平时×[X]% + 期末×[Y]%，期末未达 [Z] 分者不计平时成绩"
```

### US Patterns — Curve Traps
```
"Grades are curved to a B- median" → only ~15% can get A/A-
"Only the top [X]% of the class will receive an A"
"No curve will be applied" → raw scores stand
"The instructor reserves the right to lower grade cutoffs but not raise them"
```

### Detection Keywords
- US: "must pass both", "minimum score on final", "regardless of overall", "curve", "median"
- CN: "一票否决", "按不及格处理", "不计平时成绩", "不参与总评", "均须及格"

## E. Late Work and Make-up Traps (迟交补交陷阱)

### US Patterns
```
"No late work accepted — all late submissions receive a zero"
"Late submissions will be penalized 10% per day (including weekends)"
"Late work accepted only with documented medical or family emergency"
"No make-up exams except for university-excused absences"
"Vacation travel is not an excused absence — plan accordingly"
"Technical difficulties (computer crash, internet outage) are not valid excuses"
```

### Chinese Patterns
```
"逾期不补交，按零分计算"
"迟交作业一律不收"
"补考需持院系证明"
"不接受任何理由的补交"
"因私事请假不予补考"
```

### Detection Keywords
- US: "no late work", "zero", "per day", "including weekends", "not valid excuses"
- CN: "逾期不补", "一律不收", "不予补考", "按零分处理", "不接受任何理由"

### Policy Comparison Note
When the late policy is unusually strict, explicitly flag it. When it's generous
("two free late days", "lowest [N] grades dropped"), also note it as a green flag.

## I. Group Work Risks (小组作业雷点)

### US Patterns
```
"Peer evaluations will be factored into individual project grades"
"Students may be voted out of their group by peers for non-contribution"
"Free riders will receive a reduced grade or zero for the group component"
"Group projects account for [X]% of the grade"
```

### Chinese Patterns
```
"组内互评将影响个人成绩"
"小组分工由组员自行协调"
"组长有权对不参与组员进行扣分"
"不参与小组工作的同学该项记为0分"
```

### Detection Keywords
- US: "peer evaluation", "voted out", "free rider", "group grade", "individual adjustment"
- CN: "互评", "自行协调", "扣分", "不参与"

### Risk Assessment Cues
- High group work % (25%+) → higher risk if group dynamics are poor
- "Peer evaluation adjusts individual grade" → your grade isn't fully in your control
- "Groups will be randomly assigned" → no control over group composition
- "Group meetings required outside of class" → scheduling burden
